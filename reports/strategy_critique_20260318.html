1.1 The Zero-Audio Species Problem
28 of 234 target species have absolutely no training audio. These are: 25 "Insect sonotype" categories (47158son01–son25) and 3 amphibians (species 1491113, 25073, 517063). Any model trained only on train_audio will predict exactly 0 for these species. The only way to learn them is through soundscape supervision — yet our soundscape validation set includes these species and they contribute equally to the macro ROC-AUC metric. This directly suppresses our true competitive ceiling.

1.3 Val Set Covers Only 32% of Target Species
Our soundscape validation set covers only 75 of 234 target species. The remaining 159 species are invisible during validation. This means val_roc_auc and even holdout_roc_auc measure performance on a biased subset — species that happen to appear in the Pantanal soundscapes we have labels for. Species-level performance on the 159 invisible species is completely untracked.

What V2S Actually Tests
V2S-v2-asl fixes all of V2S-v1's shortcomings: adds ASL + dual + Perch pseudo + circ_shift.

Why it could help: V2S has 21.5M params vs B0's 5.3M → higher capacity for 234-class multi-taxa problem. BirdCLEF 2025 2nd and 5th place both used V2S as their primary backbone.

Why it might not help: BirdCLEF 2025 had 206 species from Colombia. Our 2026 dataset has 28 zero-audio insect species that no backbone can learn from train_audio alone. More parameters don't solve missing labels.

Honest estimate: If ASL + dual alone gives +0.027 to B0, V2S might add another +0.005 to +0.020 due to capacity — but this is speculative.

The Perch Ceiling Problem
When we replaced v5 (holdout 0.9192) with v9-soup (holdout 0.9467) in the ensemble, LB decreased from 0.893 to 0.892. This suggests Perch TFLite already covers most of the species the SED model improves on. Improving our SED model may give diminishing LB returns unless it covers species Perch misses — specifically the 28 zero-audio species and 25 insect sonotypes where Perch was also trained on limited data.

The path to gold likely requires either: (a) significantly better coverage of zero-audio species, or (b) a fundamentally different ensemble that goes beyond Perch.

6.1 The Insect Sonotype Post-Processing Problem
The 25 zero-audio insect sonotypes require special handling. Standard classification models will output near-zero for these species since they never appear in train_audio. The only signal comes from soundscape pseudo labels.

A targeted approach: soundscape-only training for the 25 insect sonotypes — train a separate small classifier only on soundscape clips (both labeled and Perch-pseudolabeled) for these species, then combine with the main model's predictions. This is missing from our current pipeline entirely.
