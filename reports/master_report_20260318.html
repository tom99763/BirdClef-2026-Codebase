4.3 Loss Functions
What Worked
FocalBCE hybrid BCE (w=0.61) + Focal (w=1.39, α=0.25, γ=2.0) — widely used
BCEWithLogitsLoss Outperformed FocalLoss alone in some ablations
ASL Loss Asymmetric (γ⁻=4, γ⁺=0, clip=0.05) — our best at +0.027 holdout
Dual output loss clip_loss + frame_loss equally weighted (0.5/0.5) — universal
Label smoothing 0.005 (very light); secondary labels weighted at 0.5
KD (MLDLoss) KL-divergence distill: 0.1×BCE + 0.9×MLD — 8th place
What Didn't Work
FocalLoss alone BCE hybrid consistently outperformed pure focal
OneCycleLR Performed poorly — CosineAnnealingLR preferred
High mixup alpha Limited impact beyond α=0.5 in most ablations
PCEN (our finding) Catastrophic holdout collapse (0.9271 → 0.6737) in our sedp-b0-v2. Not reported as harmful in 2025 solutions — may depend on implementation.
4.4 Data Augmentation Stack
Universal (all top solutions)
  • ✅ SpecAugment — time + freq masking (15% ratio, 2 masks)
  • ✅ Mixup — α=0.3–0.5 at 50% probability
  • ✅ Gaussian noise — std 0.005–0.025
  • ✅ Gain randomization — ±12 dB range
  • ✅ Cyclic shift — temporal invariance
Commonly Adopted
  • 🔄 Background noise mixing — SNR 5–20 dB field recordings
  • 🔄 SuMix / CutMix — additive blend at 50% prob
  • 🔄 Pink noise — realistic ambient noise injection
  • 🔄 RandomErasing — p=0.4, scale 0.02–0.33
  • 🔄 RandAugment — 2 ops, magnitude 9
Advanced (8th place+)
  • 🔷 Filter augmentation — random freq-domain band factors
  • 🔷 Pitch shifting — waveform-level augmentation
  • 🔷 Time stretching — slow/speed up audio
  • 🔷 RMS-based selection — pick highest-energy segment
  • 🔷 SWA / EMA — stochastic weight averaging

●
SED Dual Loss Architecture
AttentionSEDHead with clip + frame dual outputs. Loss = 0.5×clip + 0.5×frame. Universal in 2025 top solutions.
v5 baseline uses this. Proven +holdout vs clip-only (v12: −0.011 without).
●
GeM Frequency Pooling
Generalized Mean pooling (p=3.0 trainable) over frequency axis. Better than GlobalAveragePool for spectrograms.
Enabled since v5. use_gem=True in all configs.
●
ASL Loss
Asymmetric Loss (γ⁻=4, γ⁺=0, clip=0.05). Focuses gradient on hard negatives — ideal for severe class imbalance in multi-label bird detection.
v9: +0.027 holdout AUC vs v5 BCE. Best single technique found.
●
Model Soup (Checkpoint Avg)
Average top-k checkpoint weights. Smooths loss landscape, reduces overfitting. Consistent +0.01–0.02 holdout improvement.
save_topk_checkpoints=3 in all configs. soup scripts in scripts/model_soup.py.
●
Pseudo Labels (Multi-Round)
Perch teacher generates predictions on unlabeled soundscapes → filter by threshold → add as extra training data. 5 rounds completed.
round5_pseudo.csv: 1,176 soundscape clips. Used in v9, v23, v24.
●
Nohuman Filtering (Silero VAD)
Remove soundscape clips containing human speech. Silero VAD threshold 0.3. Universally beneficial in 2025 solutions.
Applied to all soundscape validation data. 739/1,478 clips retained.
●
Perch as Teacher/Ensemble
Google Perch TFLite used in 3 modes: pseudo-label teacher, soundscape embedding, direct classifier head. Boosts coverage for non-bird taxa.
Core of LB 0.893 ensemble. Best Perch head: val AUC 0.9748 (nohuman-label-pseudo).
●
SpecAugment
Time masking (15% ratio, 2 masks) + frequency masking (param=30). Prevents overfitting to specific temporal/spectral patterns.
Enabled in all training configs since v5.
●
Mixup
Linear interpolation of audio waveforms + labels. Regularizes decision boundaries, helps rare species.
mixup_alpha=0.5 in most configs. v22 tests alpha=0.0 (ablation).
●
GPU Mel Transform
torchaudio MelSpectrogram on GPU (60× faster than CPU librosa). Enables real-time augmentation on raw waveforms.
use_gpu_mel=True. yield_raw_audio mode in MelClipDataset.
●
Cosine LR + Warmup
CosineAnnealingLR with 3-epoch warmup. 2025 solutions confirmed OneCycleLR performs poorly.
scheduler=cosine, warmup_epochs=3 in all configs.
●
Soft Pseudo Labels / True KD
Use teacher's full probability vector (soft targets) instead of hard 0/1 labels. Transfers calibrated uncertainty and species co-occurrence.
v23 uses hard labels (pseudo augmentation). v24 queued: soft Perch 234-dim probs as direct BCE targets.
●
Circular Shift Augmentation
Randomly shift audio by a fraction of clip length. Enforces temporal invariance — especially useful for soundscapes.
circ_shift_prob=0.5 added in v23+. Not yet in v21/v22.
●
Larger Mel Resolution
2025 top solutions used n_fft=2048, n_mels=128–192. Higher resolution captures fine spectral detail.
We use n_fft=2048, n_mels=224 (above average). Not ablated independently yet.
●
Quality Filtering (min_rating)
Filter training data to min_rating ≥ 3.0 for high-quality recordings. Trade-off: data volume vs quality.
v21 (running): min_rating=3.0. v16 with warm-start: holdout dropped to 0.8227. Mixed results.
●
Temporal Smoothing at Inference
Weight adjacent 5s windows: 20% prev + 60% current + 20% next. Reduces single-frame noise in soundscape predictions.
Not explicitly implemented in current SED inference. Perch ensemble uses simple 5s stride.
●
Multi-Year Pretraining
HIGH PRIORITY
Pretrain SED on BirdCLEF 2021–2024 combined data before fine-tuning on 2026. Critical for rare species with <10 training recordings.
Not yet implemented. Requires downloading 2021–2024 datasets and taxonomy mapping. Could be highest-impact remaining improvement.
●
Larger Backbones (V2-B3 / NFNet)
IN QUEUE
EfficientNetV2-B3 or ECA-NFNet-L0 as primary backbone. 2025 dominant choice. Larger capacity helps with 234-class problem.
Chain4: B2 and B4 via embed-distill + head pipeline. B2 currently running (embed-distill-b2-v1, ep3: 0.5868).
●
Background Noise Augmentation
Mix training clips with background field recordings at SNR 5–30 dB. Simulates soundscape conditions, reduces domain gap.
noise_dir param exists in MelClipDataset but set to null. Requires a background noise corpus (e.g., ESC-50, field recordings).
●
RMS-Based Segment Selection
Among multiple crop windows, choose the highest-energy segment. More likely to contain the foreground species.
Currently random_crop() for training. Would need to add RMS scoring per window. Low implementation cost.
●
SWA / EMA Weights
Stochastic Weight Averaging or Exponential Moving Average of model weights. Complements model soup — smoother loss basin navigation.
Model soup is our current approximation. True SWA/EMA would be additive benefit.
●
Frame-Level KD Supervision
Teacher's frame-level predictions used as hard targets for student frame outputs. 8th place used teacher frame outputs at threshold 0.4.
Our dual loss supervises frames with clip-level labels. Frame-level KD from Perch would be additive.
●
PCEN (Per-Channel Energy Norm)
Learnable adaptive gain normalization. Theoretically improves SNR for weak bird calls. Used by some 2025 solutions without issue.
sedp-b0-v2-fusion: holdout collapsed 0.9271 → 0.6737 (−0.25!). Avoid until root cause understood. v23 tests PCEN again in different context.
●
CutMix
Paste random patches between two spectrograms. Successful in image classification but mixed results for audio.
v10: holdout 0.9391 but best at ep2 → overfitting. v13 (ASL+CutMix): holdout 0.8972 (−0.050 vs ASL alone). CutMix not recommended.
●
InfoNCE / Contrastive Loss
NT-Xent contrastive loss for embedding distillation. Theoretically learns more discriminative representations.
embed-distill-b0-v3: cosine sim 0.529 vs 0.628 for standard cosine loss. Batch negatives don't generalize well here.

7Recommendations & Roadmap
Immediate priority: Let v21–v24 complete and evaluate. The v23/v24 pair directly tests whether Perch pseudo labels + ASL + soft KD can push holdout beyond 0.9467 (current record). If v23 ≥ 0.95 holdout, it warrants a new ensemble submission.
Ranked Recommendations (from 2025 insights)
[1] Evaluate v23 + v24 Results — Decide Next Ensemble
If v23 or v24 holdout AUC > 0.9193: rebuild ensemble substituting it for v5. Use soup of top-3 checkpoints.
v23 (ASL + Perch hard pseudo + PCEN) early signal: val=0.7247 at ep2 > v9 val=0.7755 at ep1 (v9 ended at 0.7755). Watch if PCEN derails holdout again.
[2] Add Background Noise Augmentation
Download a background noise corpus (ESC-50, BirdCLEF 2025 unlabeled soundscapes, or raw field recordings). Set noise_dir in config. Expected: reduces soundscape domain gap. Implementation already exists — just needs a noise corpus. Low effort, potentially high impact.
[3] Multi-Year Pretraining (BirdCLEF 2021–2024)
Download BirdCLEF 2024/2025 train_audio data. Create a unified taxonomy mapping for overlapping species. Pretrain B0 SED on 2021–2024 → fine-tune on 2026 data. Critical for rare/endangered species (<10 recordings). 8th place 2025 solution attributed significant improvement to this step. Highest potential ROI.
[4] RMS-Based Segment Selection
Replace random_crop with energy-based crop selection. Score each 5s window by RMS, select top-scoring window with probability proportional to energy. Low implementation cost (~10 lines). Helps for recordings with long silences between bird calls.
[5] Temporal Smoothing at Inference
For soundscape inference, weight each 5s prediction as 20% previous + 60% current + 20% next window. Reduces single-frame false positives. Add to SED inference code and submission notebooks.
[6] Larger Backbone (V2-B3 or NFNet) — After Chain4
If embed-distill-b2/b4 shows > 0.640 cosine similarity, train full SED with that backbone. EfficientNetV2-B3 was the dominant 2025 architecture. Consider training from ImageNet without distill warmup, directly fine-tuning on 2026 data with ASL + dual loss.
[7] Full Perch KD with Frame-Level Supervision
Extend v24 concept: use Perch's frame-level probability outputs (not just clip-level) as student frame supervision. 8th place 2025 used teacher frame outputs at threshold 0.4 as hard frame targets. Requires extracting Perch frame-level predictions (currently only saving clip-level probs in round5_pseudo.csv).
[8] Investigate PCEN Root Cause
PCEN worked in some 2025 solutions but catastrophically failed in our sedp-b0-v2-fusion. Hypothesis: our trainable PCEN may have overfitting to soundscape normalization patterns not present in holdout. v23 re-tests PCEN — if holdout collapses again, disable permanently. If it holds, the culprit may be something else in sedp-v2 (e.g., LLRD, AMP fp16, a different mel config).
8Known Issues & Key Decisions
Confirmed Findings
ASL Loss is the best single technique
+0.027 holdout AUC vs BCE. No other single change matched this. ASL should be in all serious experiments.
Dual loss helps (+0.011 holdout vs clip-only)
v5 (dual BCE) vs v12 (single BCE): 0.9192 vs 0.9079. Frame-level supervision is clearly beneficial.
Holdout AUC does NOT proxy LB score
v9-asl-soup: holdout=0.9467 but LB=0.892 (worse than v5 holdout=0.9192, LB=0.893). Perch dominates the ensemble contribution.
PCEN caused catastrophic failure
sedp-b0-v2: holdout 0.9271 → 0.6737 after adding PCEN. Root cause unknown. Under investigation in v23.
CutMix degrades ASL performance
v13 (ASL+CutMix): holdout 0.8972 vs v9 (ASL alone): 0.9467. CutMix not compatible with ASL loss.
Open Questions & Hypotheses
Will soft KD (v24) beat hard pseudo labels (v23)?
Theory: soft Perch probabilities contain species co-occurrence and uncertainty info lost when thresholding. Hypothesis: soft > hard for rare species.
Why does stronger SED not improve LB?
Perch ensemble already covers most of the species detection. SED mainly fills gaps for non-Perch-covered species. The ensemble may be saturated at the Perch ceiling (~0.893).
Does min_rating filtering help with dual loss?
v21 tests this. v16 (rating=3 + warm-start) showed holdout drop to 0.8227, but that used distill backbone. v21 tests without distill warmup.
Is embedding distillation worth pursuing?
embed-distill-b0-v2 (best: 0.6283 cosine) → warmstarted SED v15 holdout: 0.9176 vs v5 baseline: 0.9192. Marginal. Chain4 tests larger backbones (B2/B4/ConvNeXt).
Submission policy: Only submit new SED ensemble when individual SED holdout AUC > 0.9193 (v5 benchmark). Current record: v9-asl-soup holdout 0.9467. Monitor v23/v24 holdout carefully — if v23 PCEN collapses again, the experiment is still useful as a confirmed negative finding.
