Validation Metrics Explained

Soundscape Val AUC

Macro ROC-AUC on a fixed set of 312 labeled clips from train_soundscapes. Measured every epoch during training. This is our primary model selection metric as it reflects cross-domain generalization from train_audio → soundscape environment. Typical range: 0.70–0.82.

Holdout AUC

Macro ROC-AUC on a separate held-out split of train_audio recordings, evaluated post-training using the best checkpoint. This measures within-domain accuracy. Typical range: 0.87–0.99. Note: The large gap between soundscape val and holdout AUC reflects genuine domain shift between controlled recordings and field soundscapes.

Key insight: Soundscape val AUC is a better proxy for competition performance than holdout AUC, because the test set consists of field soundscapes. A model with soundscape val 0.81 outperforms one with 0.78 in practice, regardless of holdout score differences.

The domain gap (holdout − soundscape val) ranges from +0.14 to +0.22 AUC units, reflecting the difficulty of generalizing from curated train_audio to field soundscapes. B0-v27-soft-boost achieves the highest holdout AUC (0.9915) despite moderate soundscape val (0.7804).

Key Findings & Ablations

✅ What Works

ASL over BCE (+0.023)
v5 BCE=0.7527 → v9 ASL=0.7755. Asymmetric focusing (γ⁻=4, γ⁺=0, clip=0.05) suppresses easy negatives more effectively in highly imbalanced 234-class settings.
Soft Knowledge Distillation (+0.030)
v9 ASL=0.7755 → v27 soft-boost=0.7804 → v28=0.8053. Perch teacher soft labels on soundscape clips bridge the domain gap: SED learns to mimic Perch's confident detections on unlabeled field recordings.
Multi-round Pseudo Labels r1–r5 (+0.008)
v28=0.8053 → v30-multipseu=0.8139. Combining 5 rounds of Perch-generated pseudo labels (1168 soft clips) provides more diverse supervision signal than single-round pseudo.
ClipMix in Dual-loss
v22 without ClipMix dropped from ~0.77 → 0.7485. ClipMix is essential for dual (clip+frame) loss configurations to prevent frame-level labels from conflicting with clip-level targets.
NS-JFT Pretraining for B0
tf_efficientnet_b0.ns_jft_in1k consistently outperforms standard ImageNet init. Noisy-student pretraining on JFT-300M provides richer low-level audio feature initialisation.

❌ What Doesn't Work

Background Noise Augmentation (−0.041)
v30-multipseu=0.8139 → v30-bgnoise=0.7725. Adding SNR [5,30] dB background noise alongside multi-round pseudo labels hurts soundscape adaptation. Pseudo labels were generated on clean audio — heavy noise creates train/inference distribution mismatch.
Soft Secondary Labels (−0.043)
v11 soft-sec=0.7098 vs v5 BCE=0.7527. Soft secondary labels introduce conflicting gradients: species that co-occur statistically may not co-occur in this specific 5-second clip.
Rating Filter ≥ 3 (−0.128)
v16 rating3=0.6250. Filtering to high-quality recordings reduces training diversity too aggressively for a 234-species task. Data quantity dominates quality at this class count.
EfficientNetV2-S (−0.043 vs B0)
V2S v1=0.7012, V2S v3-kitchen=0.7592 — both below B0 v30=0.8139. Despite larger capacity (21M params), V2S shows worse soundscape generalization. The ImageNet-21k pretraining may overfit to visual features that transfer poorly to spectrograms.
B3 Instability After Warmup
B3 best at ep2 (0.7805) then severe oscillation through ep8. With LR=5e-4 and 1536-dim features, B3's larger gradient norms destabilize training post-warmup. Holdout AUC (0.9553) remains high despite unstable soundscape val.

⚠️ Mixed / Uncertain

NPCEN Frontend (holdout +0.011, ss_val −0.051)
v26-asl-npcen: ss_val=0.7262, holdout=0.9574. NPCEN raised holdout AUC substantially but soundscape val dropped. Adaptive gain normalization may over-fit to train_audio distribution. Outcome depends on which metric is the better LB proxy.
EfficientNet-B3 (holdout 0.9553 but ss unstable)
Best per-domain score (holdout=0.9553) suggests B3 does learn strong features. The soundscape instability is an optimization problem, not a capacity problem. Lower LR (2e-4) + longer warmup (5 ep) may stabilize it. Not yet tested.
Dual BCE (clip + frame)
Dual loss adds frame-level supervision but requires ClipMix for stable training. With ClipMix, v20-dual-mixup08 reaches ~0.783. Without ClipMix, v22 drops to 0.7485. Net benefit over ASL-only unclear — ASL alone (v9) achieves 0.7755 without ClipMix dependency.
4-Fold GroupKFold (Ongoing)
fold0 best=0.8033, fold1=0.7952 (early stop), fold2=0.7656 (still training). GroupKFold should produce diverse checkpoints for model soups. Whether the soup exceeds v30-multipseu single model (0.8139) is the key question.
