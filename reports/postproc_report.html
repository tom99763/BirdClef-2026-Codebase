Executive SummaryOverview of the post-processing search

Over 53 experiment rounds totalling 1 099 distinct methods, a systematic post-processing pipeline was developed on top of Perch-based SED predictions evaluated on 59 held-out soundscape recordings (708 five-second windows each containing 234-class probability vectors). The search started from a raw Perch probe baseline of OOF AUC 0.7419 and achieved a final best of 0.8165, an absolute gain of +0.0746.

The search progressed through four distinct phases:

  1. Temporal pooling exploration (R1–R15): LogSumExp, GlobalMean blending, and entropy-weighted anchors were evaluated. Best: 0.7927.
  2. NoisyOR anchor & branch ensembling (R16–R24): NoisyOR outperformed mean anchors; first plateau at 0.8045.
  3. Richness normalization (R25–R49): SoftmaxRichness caused the largest single jump (+0.0035 at R40). A hard plateau formed at 0.8140 that resisted 9 consecutive rounds of tuning.
  4. Logit-space pre-smoothing (R50–R53): Local-max propagation in logit space, restricted to Aves classes, broke the 0.8140 plateau and lifted the best to 0.8165.

Key FindingsNumbered insights from 53 rounds of experimentation
1
Pipeline order is critical. Applying local-max propagation before SoftmaxRichness achieves 0.8163, while applying it after yields only 0.8122 — a gap of +0.0041. The SoftmaxRichness normalization alters the score distribution in a way that makes logit-space smoothing less effective when applied downstream.
2
Class-group restriction is essential for lmax_pre. Applying local-max propagation in logit space to all 234 classes gives 0.8047. Restricting to Aves classes (indices 72–233) gives 0.8163 — a +0.0116 difference. Non-Aves class predictions respond differently to temporal smoothing and must not be conflated with bird calls.
3
SoftmaxRichness is vastly superior to power-law richness normalization. The translation-invariant formulation (R40, T=0.10) produced the single largest gain in the entire experiment: +0.0035. Power-law curves (R38, AdaptRichPow) reached only +0.0016 and were dominated. The key difference is that softmax richness is calibration-free and does not require per-file threshold tuning.
4
cSEBBs is highly context-dependent. Applied standalone, cSEBBs hurts by approximately −0.025. In a pipeline with pre-smoothed inputs it contributes +0.009. The change-point detector requires stable input scores; applying it to raw probabilities causes erratic segmentation boundaries.
5
The OOF metric is noisy at small gains. With only 59 soundscape files (708 windows of 12 clips each), gains below 0.002 OOF AUC are statistically unreliable. Multiple rounds (R43–R49) saw repeated 0.8140 confirmations across different hyperparameter settings, confirming the plateau was real rather than measurement noise.
6
Temporal learnable methods and HMMs catastrophically fail. With only 12 five-second clips per soundscape file, there is insufficient temporal context to learn or infer valid transition dynamics. HMM dropped by −0.016, SoftNMS by −0.060, and VelocityAttention by up to −0.080. Methods from DCASE Task 4 assume longer recordings and cannot be naively ported.
7
NoisyOR anchor outperforms mean anchors. At R20, NoisyOR (nor_w=0.40, α=0.30) exceeded the prior best mean-anchor formulation by +0.0029. NoisyOR better models species presence under uncertain multi-label conditions: it produces a softer clip-level aggregate that is more robust to single-window outliers than arithmetic mean.

Literature Review Summary5 search rounds, ~25 papers examined across DCASE and BirdCLEF literature

Five rounds of literature search were conducted. Papers were categorized by technique, tested as faithfully as possible, and outcome recorded. The primary lesson: methods designed for DCASE Task 4 (10-second segments, evaluation by event F-score) require non-trivial adaptation and frequently fail under BirdCLEF's AUC metric and 5-second clip structure.

DCASE 2024
T4
arXiv:2406.08056, 2406.13312, 2208.09201 — Multi-scale median filter, HMM post-processing, boundary-aware smoothing, PMAM.
All failed. HMM −0.016, MultiScaleMedian −0.016, BoundaryAware −0.033. DCASE event F-score optimizes precision of onset/offset boundaries; BirdCLEF AUC optimizes ranking, making these techniques misaligned.

Conclusions & Next StepsState of the post-processing search after 53 rounds

After 53 rounds and 1 099 tested methods, the post-processing pipeline has reached a performance plateau at approximately 0.8165 OOF AUC. The final best pipeline consists of four ordered stages: logit-space local-max propagation (Aves classes only), logit scale shrink, SoftmaxRichness normalization, and cSEBBs change-point blend.

The plateau is believed to represent a ceiling imposed by the base SED model quality, not by post-processing expressiveness. Evidence for this: 9 consecutive rounds (R45–R53) tested structurally diverse methods (branch ensembles, adaptive parameters, novel segmenters) and all converged to 0.8140–0.8165, while the final breakthrough at R50 came from a new input-domain transformation (logit-space smoothing) rather than output-space manipulation.

Recommended next lever: improve the base SED model — better backbone, richer augmentation, or pseudo-label expansion — before investing further in post-processing refinement.
